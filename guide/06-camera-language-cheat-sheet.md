## Camera language cheat sheet

Seedance 2.0 is surprisingly good at understanding filmmaking terms. Using these words can instantly make your videos feel more “pro”:

### Basic camera movements

| Term | What it does | When to use it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dolly in / Push in | The camera moves forward toward the subject | Emphasize details, build tension |
| Pull back / Dolly out | The camera moves backward away from the subject | Reveal the environment, show scale |
| Pan | The camera rotates left or right | Show a wide scene, follow movement |
| Track / Truck | The camera moves sideways, parallel to the subject | Follow a subject, show space |
| Crane up/down | The camera moves up or down | Show height, add “epic” / ceremonial vibes |
| Orbit | The camera circles around the subject (partial or full orbit) | Highlight the subject, add impact |

### Advanced camera tricks

| Term | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| Hitchcock zoom (dolly zoom) | Push in while zooming out (or vice versa). The background warps for a dizzy effect. |
| One take | No cuts. A single continuous shot. |
| Follow shot | The camera moves with the main character |
| Top-down shot | Looking down from above |
| Low-angle shot | Looking up from below to make the subject feel powerful |
| Close-up | Capture details (faces, objects) |
| Wide shot | Show the full scene |
| Medium shot | Framing roughly from the knees up |

### Words to use vs. words to avoid

| ✅ Use more | ❌ Use less |
| --- | --- |
| slow, gentle, continuous | high-speed, violent, exaggerated |
| natural, smooth, stable | complex multi-person interactions |
| not stiff, no shake | heavy distortion |
