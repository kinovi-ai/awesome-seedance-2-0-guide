## FAQ + common pitfalls

### FAQ

**Q: Does upload order matter?** A: Not really. What matters is assigning each asset clearly with `@`.

**Q: Does video reference cost more?** A: Usually yes, a bit. But the quality jump is often worth it.

**Q: What if I don’t have audio?** A: You can reference the audio from a video, or describe the sound you want in the prompt.

**Q: How do I choose duration?** A:

- Normal generation: pick the total length you want

- Video extension: pick the extra seconds you want to add

**Q: My one-take keeps cutting.** A: Be explicit: “one take, no cuts for the entire clip.”

**Q: What about complex stories?** A: 5 seconds is often too short. Try 10–15 seconds.

**Q: Character consistency isn’t great.** A: Describe the character features clearly (hair, eyes, skin tone, outfit). You can often reach 95%+ consistency.

### Pitfall checklist

| ❌ Don’t do this | ✅ Do this instead |
| --- | --- |
| Too many assets (more than 8) | 5–8 is usually best. Upload the most important ones first. |
| Wrong @ labels | Double-check every label |
| Mixing up images and videos | `@image1` and `@video1` are different inputs. Label them correctly. |
| Wrong duration for extension | Generation length = extra seconds, not total length |
| Reference video is too long | Shorter reference clips are usually more accurate |
| Giving up after one try | There’s randomness. Generate a few times and pick the best. |
| Starting with something too complex | Start simple: one image + a prompt |
